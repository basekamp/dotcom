event documentation > "Team Players for Plausible Artworlds" at ICA Philadelphia

WEEK 2: CONSTRUCTING SPACES

February 3 Saturday. 3-5pm

Members of the public were invited to get engaged physically, to help re-imagine and re-form elements of the space that provided the base for our activities at the ICA. Through presentation and discussion of the concept of community spaces, examples of experimental communal architecture, and other models of supportive structures in contemporary art, an ongoing dialogue was initiated about about strategic and tactical approaches towards a spatialization of plausible artworlds.

This dialogue will continue on the project's website (www.PlausibleArtworlds.org) -- constructive criticism welcome.

"Constructing Spaces" event initiated and facilitated by Lars Fischer (Institute for Advanced Architecture, New York)
"Team Players for Plausible Artworlds" event series arranged by Basekamp
Part of "Locally Localized Graivty" an ICA, Philadelphia curated exhibition.

WEEK 6: ARCHIVES & ORGANIZATION

March 3 Saturday. 3-5pm

In the game SET, a group of people re-categorizes a group of objects. SET is 1/3 curating, 1/3 sculpture, 1/3 design. Given a collection--which may or may not already have a logical structure--and a set of ordering devices including pens and labels, the group organizes the set into multiple creative arrangements. Often the groups conflict or UPset each other, causing REset--an interesting play of revisions and discussions. Game play ends when the timer rings and the whistle is blown, leaving a surreal clash of semi-organized objects.

SET was initiated and facilitated by Marisa Jahn (Pond, San Francisco), and Erik Carver (Institute for Advanced Architecture, New York)
"Team Players for Plausible Artworlds" event series arranged by Basekamp
Part of "Locally Localized Graivty" an ICA, Philadelphia curated exhibition.
