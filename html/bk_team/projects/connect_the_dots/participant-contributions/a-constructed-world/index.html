A Constructed World - Jacqueline Riva & Geoff Lowe (Turin)

Problem
In the time leading up to his death Kurt Cobain was critical of both the music scene and system and his own withering efforts to tell-the-truth within it. What does it mean to be authentic in a market-driven world? Can artists continue to commit themselves to what they believe to be true?

Hypothesis
ACW projects stay open and see what happens. We don’t know what will happen when we bring artists together and we don’t know what the outcomes and audience reaction will be.
The ‘space’ for this exhibition and five more editions is our apartment in Turin, Italy. There are many commercial galleries and museums in Turin but not a more intimate, less formal interface (like the artist run spaces in Melbourne or New York). We want to create a more informal interface with an audience here in Turin.
In projects where we invite other artists to participate, like this show, we have often met before and already realized that we had the desire to work together. We knew most of the artists and Charlotte had met the other artists who were invited.

Materials & Resources
We aim to make low-fi – low-budget high-energy projects. Our budget extends to postage and freight, that’s about it. For A level of trust we used our own domestic equipment for sound and video. We work with like-minded people in a situation where we can do something quickly and impulsively with limited funds and include artists who make fantastic work. Rather than waiting for bureaucratic institutions to put artists in shows we are able to include artists from other countries quite spontaneously.

Procedure
What we see as resources are things like not-knowing and embarrassment.
Previous contacts, shared resources, inviting people who don’t-know in an impulsive, informal setting. The performance, by Viliani and Robecchi, was initiated by them, both art critics, not artists. It was decided that ACW would create the context for their performance given that they had not performed before.
Everything, artists bios, images and other material was organized directly through the artists and not through any representative galleries.

Data
The space: More Fools in Town
via Guastalla 19
10124 Turin
aconstructedworld@libero.it
Exhibition title: A level of trust. After Kurt Cobain.
Dates: 21 May – 5 June 2004
Coordinated by: A Constructed World, artists Jacqueline Riva + Geoff Lowe and curator Charlotte Laubard
The artists: Ester Partegas, New York, vedovamazzei, Milan, Mungo Thomson, Los Angeles, Arnaud Maguet, Nice, Elizabeth Newman, Melbourne, Piero Golia, Naples, Blair Trethowan, Melbourne, Olav Westphalen, New York.
Performance: Untitled, Andrea Viliani and Michele Robecchi.(both curator/critic)
Email conversation: Laubard facilitated an email conversation related to the show, ‘What does it mean to be an ‘authentic’ artist now-a-days?’
Conversation participants from the exhibition: Elizabeth Newman, Blair Trethowan Mungo Thomson, Laubard and ACW. Others included curator/critic Alessandro Rabottini, Milan, and gallerists Jarrod Rawlins, Uplands Gallery Melbourne and Hu Fang, Vitamin Creative Space, Guangzhou.

Results
We reached a large audience at the opening, about 100 people, and even though the space is just 4 ante-rooms in our upstairs apartment, numerous people from Milan, Turin and Sydney approached us to show at More Fools in Town. One man came with paintings in the back of his car and we looked at them in the street. Some brought and sent us slides and others people emailed images.
There is dvd documentation of the performance by Viliani and Robecchi and photographic documentation of the works. The exhibition was reviewed in Torino Sette, a weekly arts and entertainment newspaper, at Exibart on the internet and has subsequently been written about in Flash Art. We also received emails from friends and acquaintances offering encouragement for the project.
Three works in the show sold to collectors which is important for this project as the commission will fund the next edition of More Fools in Town, a show called Fully Functional opening late September.

Conclusions
ACW values not-knowing as a shared space. We do not anticipate outcomes and do not work in an empirical mode. Our projects use authenticity to build trust, whether it’s possible to believe in trust or not. We allow absence to exist in the project, for example Viliani and Robecchi hadn’t made a performance before, nor did they rehearse. Just as the audience may be apprehensive or embarrassed the gallerists and performers were unused to their roles and unsure what would happen.
